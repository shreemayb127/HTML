Hydrogen(H)
1. Atomic Number:1
2. Symbol: H
3. Properties: Hydrogen is the lightest and most abundant element in the universe. It is a colorless, odorless, and highly flammable gas.
4. Uses: Hydrogen is used in fuel cells, as a rocket propellant, and in the production of ammonia for fertilizers.
5. Occurrence: It is found in water (H₂O), hydrocarbons, and as a diatomic molecule (H₂) in the atmosphere.


Helium (He)
1.Atomic Number: 2
2.Symbol: He
3.Properties: Helium is a colorless, odorless, tasteless, non-toxic, inert gas. It has the lowest boiling and melting points of all the elements.
4.Uses: Helium is used in balloons, as a cooling medium for nuclear reactors and MRI machines, and in deep-sea breathing systems.
5.Occurrence: It is primarily extracted from natural gas deposits and is the second most abundant element in the universe.
